<h1> I Really Hate Posters So This Is What You Are Getting Instead </h1>

Hello! Thank you for coming to my presentation. I will level with you: some academic work is not poster-able, such as... an argument for looking at art     differently. In their place, this is what I would like you to do instead.

1. Read the following questions:
 - When you look at an artwork, **what do you see?**
 - Look again. **Try not to interpret what you're seeing. What do you see?**
 - Look again. **What resists interpretation in what you're seeing?**
 - Look again. **How does it make you feel?**

2. With these questions in mind, visit this [online collection](https://web.archive.org/web/20220222042410/https://smartcollection.uchicago.edu/collections/84055/ambiguous-beauty-curated-by-seth-nguyen) that I curated from the Smart's collection.
3. Look at the art.
4. Read the poster, if you want, [here](fishspeech.github.io/docs/assets/Poster.pdf).
5. Come back to the [Zoom room](https://uchicago.zoom.us/j/98483849506?pwd=WTdrWHF2WEc4UlR0cTFyL3VReDdJdz09). Tell me about what you saw and felt!
